Language of the Wind
The wind, a whispering voice so sly Sings its song across the sky It tells a tale of places far Where mountains rise and eagles are Its voice is soft, its tone is sweet A soothing balm to tired feet It speaks in tongues of foreign lands And carries tales from desert sands It tells of love and of despair Of secrets whispered in the air It speaks of hopes and of dreams And all the wondrous things it’s seen The wind is wild, it cannot be tamed Its power cannot be restrained But when it speaks, we hear the call And follow it to where it may fall So let us listen to the wind And hear the secrets that it’s been For in its voice we may yet find The language of the heart and mind. -Manpreet Singh
